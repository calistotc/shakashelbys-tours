Garden Route

The Garden Route is a stunning stretch along the southern coast of South Africa, offering a delightful blend of natural beauty, diverse landscapes, and exciting attractions. Spanning approximately 300 kilometers (186 miles) from Cape Town to Port Elizabeth, this scenic route is a paradise for every traveler.

With its picturesque coastline, lush forests, charming towns, and abundant outdoor activities, the Garden Route Destinations captivate visitors worldwide. Whether you’re exploring the stunning Tsitsikamma National Park, taking in the panoramic views from the Knysna Heads, or hiking through the enchanting Robberg Nature Reserve, you’ll be surprised by awe-inspiring sights and experiences along this route.

Not only does the Garden Route offer spectacular scenery, but it also boasts a range of activities during your South African Tour. So, join our guided Garden Route Tours where every mile brings new discoveries and memories.

The Garden Route is a scenic stretch offering natural beauty, diverse landscapes, and various highlights as well. Here, we have mentioned the top Garden Route Tourist Attractions:

 

  • The Garden Route boasts stunning coastal attractions including dramatic cliffs, pristine beaches, and lush forests.
  • The Tsitsikamma National Park is a must-visit destination along Garden Route.
  • The charming town Knysna is one of the top Garden Route Attractions. It is nestled on the shores of a picturesque lagoon and surrounded by indigenous forests.
  • Known for its pristine beaches, Plettenberg Bay bowls a range of water-based activities.
  • Robberg Nature Reserve is a protected peninsula, located near Plettenberg Bay.
  • Oudtshoorn is a town located in the Western Cape province of South Africa, situated along the Garden Route.
  • A historic coastal town, Mossel Bay is one of the major Garden Route Destinations.

 

 

The Garden Route in South Africa offers a plethora of exciting activities for visitors to enjoy. And here are some of the top Things To Do At Garden Route:

 

  • Encounter Wildlife: Visit the various wildlife sanctuaries during the Garden Route Trips. These include Monkeyland, Birds of Eden, and Tenikwa Wildlife Awareness Centre. Moreover, you can observe primates, birds, and big cats up close.
  • Explore Wilderness National Park: Wilderness National Park is a haven for birdwatchers, kayakers, and nature lovers too. So, visit this park and enjoy activities like paddling, hiking or swimming in one of the park’s many rivers and lakes.
  • Discover the Cango Caves: The Cango Caves are located near the town of Oudtshoorn. And, you can take a guided tour through the various chambers, and see the incredible formations during your Garden Route Tours.
  • Enjoy the beaches: Furthermore, the Garden Route is blessed with beautiful beaches. And, you can spend a day soaking up the sun and swimming in the turquoise waters of Plettenberg Bay, Buffalo Bay, or Victoria Bay.
  • Go whale watching: For Whale watching, Garden Route is a prime location, particularly from June to November. Head to Hermanus, a short drive from the route, and witness the majestic Southern Right Whales.
  • Hike the Otter Trail: Take a legendary Otter Trail, a five-day coastal hike. It’ll take you through stunning landscapes, indigenous forests, and along the rugged shoreline too.
  • Wine Tasting: Along with other Garden Route Activities, you can also explore the Garden Route’s emerging wine region. Visit vineyards and wineries offering tastings and cellar tours.
  • Enjoy Water Sports: Engage in a variety of water-based activities such as surfing, kayaking, paddleboarding, or sailing along the coast. 

The Best Time To Visit Garden Route is during the spring and autumn months, from September to November and March to May. During this period, the weather is mild, and the region is less crowded with tourists.

You can expect pleasant temperatures, sunny days, and less rainfall, making it ideal for various outdoor activities. However, Garden Route Tours can be done year-round, and each season offers its own unique charm.

Garden Route Travel FAQS

What is the Garden Route?

The Garden Route is a scenic stretch along the southern coast of South Africa, renowned for its natural beauty, diverse landscapes, and attractions. It offers a combination of stunning coastlines, lush forests, charming towns, and abundant outdoor activities.

How long is the Garden Route?

The Garden Route extends for approximately 300 kilometers (186 miles), starting from Mossel Bay in the Western Cape and ending in Storms River in the Eastern Cape.

What are the must-see attractions on the Garden Route?

Must-see Attractions Of Garden Route include Tsitsikamma National Park, Knysna Heads, Robberg Nature Reserve, Cango Caves, Storms River Mouth, and the Map of Africa viewpoint. These sites showcase the region’s natural wonders, wildlife, and stunning vistas.

How do I travel along the Garden Route?

The Garden Route is easily accessible by road. You can either self-drive or hire a car to explore the region at your own pace. There are also organized tours and shuttle services available for transportation between towns and attractions.

Where does the Garden Route start and end?

The Garden Route starts in Mossel Bay and ends in Storms River, South Africa.

How long does it take to drive along the Garden Route?

It typically takes around 5-7 days to drive along the Garden Route, depending on the Places To Visit On Garden Route.

What are some of the top attractions in Garden Route?

Top Garden Route Tourist Attractions include the Tsitsikamma National Park, Knysna Heads, Cango Caves, and the Wilderness National Park.

Is Garden Route suitable for a self-drive trip?

Yes, the Garden Route is highly suitable for a self-drive trip, offering flexibility and the opportunity to explore at your own pace.

What time of the year can I experience Garden Route the best?

The Garden Route can be enjoyed throughout the year, but the best time is generally from September to April when the weather is warm and rainfall is lower.

Can I visit Garden Route as a day trip from Cape Town?

While it’s possible, visiting the Garden Route as a Day Trip From Cape Town is not recommended due to the distance involved. It’s better to plan at least a few days to fully experience the area.
